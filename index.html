About Me

Hello! My name is Tülin Varol Mete and I have a PhD in high energy physics from the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. During the second half of my Ph.D. and my postdoctoral tenure (2011-2022), I was based at Geneva, Switzerland as a member of the ATLAS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at the European Organisation for Nuclear Research (CERN).

Officially, my job was to conduct data analyses to infer meaningful insights for new physics searches. It's actually a fancy way of saying to dredge up something interesting from the wreckage of two protons after they collide in a cavity that is as empty as interplanetary space and colder than outer space, literally in the coolest place in the universe!

Working as a physicist at CERN, I gained a wide-range of valuable experiences, including working with peta-byte scale data, utilizing cloud computing platforms and machine learning techniques, developing problem solving skills and implementing various statistical methods. I also served as a run coordinator for the ATLAS detector's Liquid Argon (LAr) calorimeter operation team and also as an online data quality expert for Global Monitoring. Particularly in these roles, I enhanced my leadership skills and strategic decision-making in high-pressure and time-critical settings.

In 2022, I relocated to the western suburbs of Chicago due to family circumstances, where I am currently based. During the time I have not been working, I developed a strong interest in quantum computation and have been actively learning through online platforms. My academic background is rooted in high-energy physics, and the idea of applying the principles that govern the subatomic world to computational systems deeply fascinates me. Recently, I’ve been exploring exciting opportunities that will challenge me and allow me to further develop my skills.

If you are interested, you can find links to my GitHub and LinkedIn profiles on the navigation bar up top. Feel free to get in touch with me. I would be happy to meet unique minds, exchange ideas, share my experiences and learn from your experiences.
